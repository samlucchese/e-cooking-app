Cooking vs. Baking


These two terms are terms that we hear all the time. However, do you know the difference between the two? Many people cannot answer this question or they will tell you that they are the exact same thing. But they are wrong... kind of.

Cooking Baking
A method of preparing food by using heat. A method of cooking food by using dry heat, usually in an oven.
Usually, we "cook" meals, meat, fish, and vegetables. Usually, we "bake" bread, cake, and desserts.
Considered an "art", measurements are not as important. Considered a "science", measurements need to be precise.

What is Cooking?


Let's start with the definition. Cooking is defined as "the practice or skill of preparing food by combining, mixing, and heating ingredients".

While cooking, the chemical properties of the ingredients are manipulated through heat, changing the appearance, texture, and/or flavor. As a result, ingredients can soften or harden as the fibers are broken down or expand.

We cook using one or more of the methods listed below. You will notice that baking is the first cooking method on this list.

  • Baking
  • Grilling
  • Frying
  • Roasting
  • Simmering
  • Smoking
  • Sauteing
  • Boiling

    • What is Baking?


    Again, let's start with the definition. Baking is defined as "the method of preparing food using dry heat, typically in an oven".

    We can technically bake many things, including bread, desserts, fish, poultry, meats, and vegetables. But many times when people consider themselves bakers, they are referring to desserts and bread.

    When we bake, we use continuous dry heat to cook the food. Without the constant exposure to dry heat, the chemical composition of some of the foods cannot change.



    Art vs. Science

    Another thing that many people do not know is that when it comes to cooking and baking, cooking is often referred to as an art, while baking is considered a science.

    This means, that when we are cooking, we are essentially free to do whatever we like and cater to our individual preferences. For example, if you are grilling a cheeseburger and you decide you want extra cheese, 1 or even 2 extra slices are not going to totally ruin or alter the taste of the dish. Also, substitutions can easily be made with ingredients.

    With baking however, you have to be much more careful, just like a scientist would be. When we bake, it isnt as simple as "Oh I love sugar so I'll add double the sugar!" If we did this, it would totally ruin the dish. Dry heat affects the ingredients differently than if we cooked something on the stovetop and we must be careful to measure the ingredients correctly and accurately. If we make a mistake here, it will almost certainly affect the outcome.


    Conclusion: Cooking vs. Baking


    1. What is the difference between cooking and baking?

    • Cooking is an art form where we prepare food by using heat. It is limitless, can be done a number of ways, and is customizable to your preferences. Cooking is a broad category of cooking methods and baking is included in one of these methods.

    • Baking is a science, and it is a method of cooking where we apply even temperature to cook food. Instructions must be followed closely and carefully. While cooking can be done many ways, baking can only be done using indirect, dry heat.

    2. Is one better than the other?

    • While baking is considered one of the healthiest cooking methods, that doesnt necessarily mean it is better than cooking overall. It just depends on the dish you are trying to make, some foods taste better cooked with methods other than baking.

    • The only reason people classify baking as healthier is because there are very little additives. Depending on the recipe and the ingredients involved, you may find that your recipe isn't really all that healthy.
